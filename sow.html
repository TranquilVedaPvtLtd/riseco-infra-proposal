1Scope and deliverables

The Provider will deliver the following four workstreams for the Client's existing internal application (the "Application"):

1.1 Security audit and hardening

  • Assessment of authentication, session handling, access control, data exposure, secrets handling, input validation, and hosting configuration.
  • Implementation of role-based access control (director, site, and office roles).
  • Prioritised remediation of identified findings and secure hosting configuration.
  • Acceptance: a written findings report is delivered, agreed high and medium findings are fixed, and one re-test confirms closure.

1.2 Feature build on the Application

  • Strengthening of estimate-versus-actual reconciliation on indents.
  • Director flagging on budget overruns.
  • Drawing and document management with version history and timestamped receipt.
  • Polish of petty cash, sales, and assets screens.
  • Acceptance: the feature list is locked in writing in week one; each feature is accepted against that agreed list.

1.3 WhatsApp assistant ("Riseco Assistant")

  • A WhatsApp interface for conversational queries answered from live Application data.
  • A scheduled 6:00 AM daily brief covering all sites, open alerts, and the day's priority actions.
  • Real-time alerts for budget overruns and pending approvals.
  • Custom skills for the Client's workflows (reconciliation, site reports, indent status, petty cash).
  • Least-privilege data access and an audit log of every action the assistant performs.
  • Acceptance: the assistant answers a defined set of queries correctly and delivers a 6:00 AM brief on a live schedule.

1.4 Hosting and go-live

  • Migration of the Application off the free tier to production hosting on the Client's own cloud account.
  • Server provisioning for the assistant, backups, and monitoring.
  • Acceptance: the Application and assistant are live on the Client's account with backups and monitoring confirmed.

2Exclusions

  • A full rebuild or re-platforming of the Application is not included; this engagement secures and extends the existing system.
  • Conversion of the Application into a multi-tenant product for resale to third parties is not included and is a separate future engagement.
  • Placement of full-time engineers on the Client's team is not included; this is fixed-scope work.
  • Cloud, hosting, and AI usage charges are not included and are billed directly to the Client's own account at actual cost, with no mark-up.
  • New modules outside the agreed feature list are quoted separately and begin only on the Client's written sign-off.

4Change requests

Any work outside this Statement of Work follows a written flow: the Client submits a written request, the Provider returns an impact estimate (cost and time), the Client signs off, and only then does work begin. No out-of-scope work is performed or billed without prior written sign-off.

Revisions

5Acceptance and timeline

Each deliverable is accepted against the acceptance criteria stated in section 1. The Client has 7 business days from delivery to review and respond.

Deemed acceptance: if the Client does not respond within 7 business days of a deliverable being delivered, that deliverable is treated as accepted.

Indicative timeline: security audit begins within 3 working days of sign-off; go-live is targeted within approximately 6 weeks, subject to timely access and Client decisions.

6Ownership, confidentiality, and continuity

  • Intellectual property: all custom code written for the Client under this engagement is work-for-hire and becomes the Client's property on full payment. The Client receives the repository, credentials, and a runbook. The Provider may, after the Client's written consent, reference the engagement as a case study.
  • Data: the Client's drawings, financial data, and vendor information remain on the Client's own cloud account.
  • Confidentiality: each party keeps the other's non-public information confidential. This is cross-referenced to any separate non-disclosure agreement the parties sign.
  • Continuity: on completion the Provider delivers a runbook and a two-hour handover call so the Client can operate and change the system independently.

7Liability, termination, and jurisdiction

  • Liability cap: the Provider's total liability is capped at the fees paid by the Client in the three months preceding the claim.
  • Termination: either party may terminate the maintenance engagement with 30 days written notice. The one-time build fee is non-refundable once work has begun; maintenance is charged pro-rata for the termination month. On termination the Client retains the running system, code, and credentials.
  • Force majeure: neither party is liable for delays caused by events beyond its reasonable control.
  • Jurisdiction: this Statement of Work is governed by the laws of India. Courts at Pune, Maharashtra have jurisdiction.
  • Arbitration: disputes not resolved amicably are referred to arbitration seated at Pune under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.
